We want to solve look at the sign of the expression

$$ \Delta(p, q, r) = \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{p}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{r}\right) - \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{q}\right) $$

as \(p\), \(q\) and \(r\) vary. This'll give us an idea of what space each honeycomb lives in.

\(\mathbb{S}^3\)

Firstly we can look at the spherical case - \(\mathbb{S}^3\). In this case we want \(\Delta(p, q, r) > 0\) i.e.

$$ \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{p}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{r}\right) \gt \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{q}\right) $$

We can get some quick bounds for the LHS as it takes a maximum value of \(\frac{3}{4} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \) for \(p = r = 3\) (\(\sin(\pi/x)\) is decreasing. We can get a lower bound of \(\frac{1}{2}\) for \(q=3\) as \(\cos(\pi/x)\) is an increasing function. Sumarising

$$ \begin{align} \frac{1}{2} & \lt \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{p}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{r}\right) & \lt \frac{3}{4} \\ q = 3 & & p = r = 3 \\ & & q = \frac{\pi}{\arccos(\frac{3}{4})} \approx 4.34 \end{align} $$

Therefore \(3 \leq q \leq 4.34\) which further gives \(q = 3\) or \(q = 4\). We can now solve \(\Delta(p, q, r) > 0\) in each of these cases:

\(q = 3\)

For \(q = 3\) we want to solve

$$ \frac{1}{2} \lt \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{p}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{r}\right) \leq \frac{3}{4} $$

This inequality gives \(p, r \lt 6\) as a bound. Testing each of the possibilities gives the solutions:

$$ \boxed{\{3,3,3\}, \{3,3,4\}, \{3,3,5\}, \{4,3,3\}, \{5,3,3\}} $$

\(q = 4\)

For \(q = 4\) we want to solve

$$ \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \lt \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{p}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{r}\right) \leq \frac{3}{4} $$

This inequality gives \(p, r \lt 4\) as a bound giving the solution:

$$ \boxed{\{3,4,3\}} $$

\(\mathbb{E}^3\)

In the Euclidean case we want to solve \(\Delta(p, q, r) = 0\) which can be done in a similar fashion to the spherical case. It turns out that we get the same inequalities but the only solution is

$$ \boxed{\{4,3,4\}} $$

\(\mathbb{H}^3\)

Finally we want to solve \(\Delta(p, q, r) \le 0\). This encompasses all of the triples\(\{p,q,r\}\) not already mentioned.
